# A Real Ending, a Better First Day, and a World That Talks Back

Hey car folks,

Four days, one big idea running through all of it: the game should feel
alive from the first frame to the last one. So we rebuilt both ends of the
run and a lot of the middle. Here is everything that landed.

---

## The finale finally lives up to the build

Winning Nationals used to hand you a placement and a couple of cards. Now
it is a proper ending, a full ceremony that knows your story.

- It opens on a black screen and three slow lines that change depending on
  how long the road was. Win it your first season and the game calls you a
  prodigy. Grind it out over four and it says so. Come back and do it twice
  and you get the dynasty cut.
- The car in the spotlight is your actual build, not a stock photo. If it
  ever gave up a secret in the garage, the ceremony remembers.
- There is a bookend back to the barn where it all started, a credits roll
  that stars your car and everyone who was in your story by name, a quiet
  beat in the empty garage after the trophy is on the shelf, and one last
  shot of the next project waiting under a fresh tarp.
- On the drive home the phone lights up. The wife texts. Buck texts. What
  they say depends on how the season actually went.

We also drew three brand new pieces of art just for this: the restored car
back in the barn doorway at golden hour, the empty garage at midnight, and
the next tarp at dawn. Go win something and see it.

---

## The first five minutes got the same love

We sat down and watched how a brand new player actually experiences the
opening, and fixed every place it tripped.

- **One card, then wrenching.** The old intro made you read five screens
  about fuel, loans, Buck, and Nationals before you touched a single bolt.
  Now it is one card: she is on jack stands, get her running. Every other
  rule shows up the first time it actually matters. Buck introduces himself
  by text when show season opens. The pay schedule explains itself on your
  first payday.
- **The Bedside Box.** Every old truck hides something. Right after you get
  her home, you find a steel toolbox bolted to the bed side, locked, no key
  anywhere on the property. Pry it open and you are into your first real
  tools: a set of made in USA wrenches older than you are and a trouble
  light that still works. No more staring at a job list wondering what to
  buy first.
- **The garage fills in as you earn it.** A new player used to land on a
  wall of panels all at once. Now the side boards show up when they mean
  something: your work orders appear when you start your first job, the
  calendar appears once your first week is in the books.

---

## The world talks back

The scene around you is a lot louder and a lot more personal now.

- **The radio and the news react to you.** The ticker at the bottom is no
  longer just flavor. It pulls from what you actually did, your results,
  your rivalry with Buck, your following. And when the scene is genuinely
  talking about you, the whole thing changes shape and lights up gold so
  you cannot miss your moment.
- **Your phone gets messages.** The people in your world text you after
  things happen. Buck copes or gloats depending on the head to head.
  Rivals who warm up to you slide into the DMs. The wife checks in when the
  budget swings too far one way or the other.
- **The Scene Annual.** Every season now wraps with a newspaper front page
  that writes your year up, headline and all, from champion to also ran.
- **The scene remembers.** Buyers reference your reputation when they make
  offers. The title screen tracks your career. Milestone weeks get their
  own headline in the recap.

---

## The quiet weeks count too

Not every week is a wrench week. Sometimes all you get done is work, the
kids, dinner, and a late night video about the next job while the truck
sits patient on the stands. That is not failing, that is the lifestyle. So
those weeks get a voice now, a warm one, and the homework you did watching
build videos even earns you a little something. It makes the Saturdays you
do get in the garage land that much harder.

And when a Saturday meet rolls by before you have earned your way into
shows, the world moves on without you and tells you so. Your day is coming.

---

## Balance, and one nasty bug

We ran full seasons end to end to see how the money really flows, and made
two changes so the climb feels fair.

- **Your following grows a lot faster.** Content used to take forever to
  snowball, which meant the payouts and sponsors that come with a big
  audience showed up seasons too late. Posting now builds real momentum,
  so the hustle side of the game pays off on the timeline it should.
- **Dale takes care of regulars.** The serious, show ready work is meant to
  go through the shop in your early seasons, so we made loyalty matter.
  Keep bringing Dale your business and his rate keeps dropping, all the way
  to a quarter off once you are a true regular. He will wave you off at the
  register when you cross a tier.

We also hunted down a bug that could freeze your week the moment your
following got big. If you ever had the game lock up right around your
second or third show, that was it, and it is gone.

---

## The little stuff

- Every em-dash in the entire game is gone. The whole thing reads in one
  clean voice now.
- The cinematic bars on cut scenes got slimmed way down, so scenes fill the
  frame instead of looking zoomed out.
- All the cut scenes got their slow, subtle drift restored, so nothing sits
  dead still.

---

That is the four day stretch. Both ends of the run feel like a real story
now, and the scene in between actually knows your name. Thanks as always
for the notes and the save files. Go pull the cover off something.
